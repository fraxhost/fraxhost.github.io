Greetings!
I’m Ahmed Ryan, a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Alabama, working as a Graduate Research Assistant under Dr. Md Rayhanur Rahman. Prior to joining academia, I spent three years as a Software Engineer, including a tenure at Cefalo developing secure web solutions for European clients. I graduated with a Bachelor’s in Software Engineering from the Institute of Information Technology, University of Dhaka.
My research focuses on the intersection of Software Supply Chain Security, Malware Analysis, and Artificial Intelligence. I have developed statement-level taxonomies for malicious Python packages and evaluated Large Language Models (LLMs) for fine-grained threat indicator identification. My work further explores automating Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTP) extraction and the capability of Large Language Models (LLMs) for fine-grained threat indicator identification. Additionally, I have investigated AI-assisted formal verification.
Research Interest Areas:
  • Software Supply Chain Security (SSCS)
  • Malware Analysis & Detection
  • AI for Software Engineering (AI4SE)
  • Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI)
  • Formal Verification
